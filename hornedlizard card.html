Horned Lizard

By restricting blood flow leaving the head horned lizards are able to shoot blood out of their eyes to ward off predators.

  • Scientific Name: Phrynosoma
  • Average Length: 5 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 5 years
  • Habitat: Desert

Horned Lizards are also commonly refered to as horned toads. They are well adapted to arid desert climates and their color acts as a camouflage. When threatened a horned lizard will puff up to appear harder to swallow and, as a last resort a horned lizard will shoot blood out of it's eyes.
